// How it works

Not monitoring.
Prediction.

Every monitoring tool shows you what happened. CVIS tells you what's about to happen — and why.

01 — COLLECT
OS-agnostic metric collection
psutil reads CPU, memory, disk, network, and processes every 2 seconds across Linux, Windows, and macOS — no agents, no config.
02 — LEARN
3-model ML ensemble trains live
PyTorch LSTM detects trends. β-VAE finds reconstruction anomalies. Isolation Forest catches statistical outliers. All three run in parallel, continuously.
03 — REMEMBER
Failure DNA engine
Every incident trains a pattern. After 7+ occurrences, CVIS recognises the pre-failure signature with 87–94% accuracy and 95% confidence.
04 — WARN
Plain English alerts — 30 min early
"OOM predicted in 29 minutes — seen this pattern 16 times on this machine." Not a raw number. A sentence you can act on.

Auto-Adaptation Layer.
No config. Ever.

CVIS detects what machine it's running on and automatically adjusts every threshold, every model sensitivity, every alert rule — without you touching a config file.

  • Hardware Fingerprinting
    Detects CPU cores, RAM, SSD vs HDD, battery presence, virtualisation, Docker — in under 100ms on first launch.
  • Adaptive Thresholds
    8GB laptop gets OOM warnings at 75%. 64GB workstation gets them at 88%. Same software, different intelligence.
  • Baseline Learning
    After 30 minutes of watching your machine, CVIS sets thresholds based on YOUR normal — not industry averages.
  • Zero False Alarms
    If your machine always runs at 70% CPU, that's normal for you. CVIS learns this and stops crying wolf.
